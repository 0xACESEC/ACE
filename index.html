My day-to-day has involved: Breaking into web applications to uncover vulnerabilities before the bad guys do Reversing and analyzing iOS and Android apps for security flaws Running internal and external network assessments, identifying misconfigurations, lateral movement paths, and more Planning and executing red team engagements — simulating APT-style attacks to test detection and response capabilities

What I Do

  • Penetration testing

    As a penetration tester, I conduct thorough assessments by simulating real-world cyberattacks on web applications, mobile platforms, and network infrastructures. My goal is to identify vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and weaknesses before malicious actors can exploit them. Through manual testing, automated tools, and in-depth exploitation techniques, I help organizations strengthen their defenses and improve their security posture.

  • Red teaming

    As a red teamer, I conduct full-scope adversary simulations, mimicking advanced, stealthy cyberattacks to assess an organization's detection, response, and overall resilience. This includes evading security controls, exploiting vulnerabilities across networks, applications, and physical access points, and testing the effectiveness of incident response protocols. By emulating real-world threat actors, I help organizations uncover gaps in their defenses and improve their ability to detect and mitigate sophisticated attacks.

  • Exploit Development

    Malware development involves the creation of malicious software specifically designed to exploit security vulnerabilities, bypass protective measures, and disrupt or control systems. This includes developing custom payloads for data exfiltration, remote access, privilege escalation, or system manipulation. I focus on understanding how different types of malware (such as ransomware, rootkits, or trojans) interact with target systems, and I use this knowledge to better understand attack vectors and improve defensive strategies for organizations.

  • Capture The Flag (CTF)

    Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions are immersive cybersecurity challenges where participants solve a variety of puzzles, exploit vulnerabilities, and complete tasks designed to simulate real-world hacking scenarios. These challenges cover a broad spectrum of skills, including reverse engineering, cryptography, web exploitation, and forensics. Through CTFs, I sharpen my problem-solving abilities, enhance my technical knowledge, and stay updated with the latest attack techniques and security tools, all while competing against others in a fun, fast-paced environment.
